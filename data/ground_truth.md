These are recording/transcription problems in the raw export. None of them
represent real financial events; all should be fixed during ingestion, before
any variance analysis runs.

- **BU name typos/casing** (7 rows): 2024-12 Production -> "Producton"; 2024-05 Back-Office -> "Back Office"; 2025-02 Digital -> "DIGITAL "; 2026-04 Digital -> "Digitial"; 2025-07 Marketing -> "marketing"; 2026-06 Back-Office -> "BackOffice"; 2024-09 Back-Office -> "BackOffice". - **Missing Opex values** (9 cells, blank in the CSV): 2025-06 Marketing / opex_facilities_actual; 2024-07 Back-Office / opex_it_actual; 2024-01 Back-Office / opex_travel_budget; 2024-04 Production / opex_marketing_actual; 2026-02 Back-Office / opex_marketing_budget; 2025-01 Marketing / opex_travel_actual; 2024-04 Marketing / opex_facilities_budget; 2025-11 Digital / opex_it_budget; 2025-02 Production / opex_travel_actual. - **Currency-formatted text amounts** (10 cells stored as strings like "EUR120,000" instead of a plain number): 2025-05 Marketing / cogs_actual; 2025-05 Production / payroll_budget; 2024-12 Digital / cogs_budget; 2025-10 Production / revenue_budget; 2025-01 Back-Office / opex_facilities_actual; 2025-11 Marketing / revenue_prior_year; 2024-08 Production / opex_facilities_budget; 2026-06 BackOffice / revenue_budget; 2025-12 Digital / opex_it_budget; 2026-05 Production / payroll_budget. - **DATA ENTRY TRAP — Production / 2025-11 / revenue_actual**: recorded as EUR52,243,584, an extra trailing zero fat-fingered during manual entry. True value is EUR5,224,358. This is NOT a real business anomaly — at ~10x the normal monthly run-rate for a single BU (more than half the company's typical *annual* revenue), it fails a basic magnitude sanity check against trailing months and against COGS/payroll for the same period, which stayed normal. Correct by dividing by 10 back to EUR5,224,358 before any variance analysis.
- **Inconsistent date formats** in the `month` column: mixes ISO (YYYY-MM-DD), US (MM/DD/YYYY), long-form ("Month YYYY"), and year/month (YYYY/MM) — 25 rows as format #0, 31 rows as format #1, 32 rows as format #2, 32 rows as format #3. All represent the first day of the month and must be normalized to a single date type.
- **Duplicate rows** (4 month/BU combinations entered twice): 01/01/2025 Marketing; 2025-06-01 Marketing; April 2026 Digitial; March 2026 Digital. Must be de-duplicated before aggregation to avoid double-counting.

## 3. Why the trap matters

The data entry trap (see above) is deliberately similar in *shape* to a real
anomaly — a large, unexplained jump in a single BU/month — but is
distinguishable by magnitude: it implies a single BU produced in one month
more revenue than several months of its entire annual budget, with no
corresponding movement in COGS or payroll. A later agent that flags variances
purely on percentage deviation without a magnitude/plausibility check would
misreport this as a business anomaly. It should instead be caught and
corrected during ingestion/QA, before it ever reaches the Variance Agent.
